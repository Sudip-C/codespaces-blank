Biology is the scientific study of life.

Living organisms include animals, plants, fungi, microorganisms, and many other forms of life.

Biology studies how organisms are structured, how they function, how they reproduce, how they interact with their environment, and how they evolve.

All living organisms share certain characteristics.

Living organisms are composed of one or more cells.

They use energy.

They maintain internal conditions.

They respond to their environment.

They grow and develop.

They reproduce.

They contain genetic information.

Cells are the basic structural and functional units of life.

Some organisms consist of a single cell.

These organisms are called unicellular organisms.

Other organisms are composed of many specialized cells.

These organisms are called multicellular organisms.

A cell is surrounded by a cell membrane.

The cell membrane controls the movement of many substances into and out of the cell.

The cell membrane helps maintain the internal environment of the cell.

The cytoplasm is the region inside the cell where many chemical reactions occur.

Eukaryotic cells contain a nucleus.

The nucleus stores most of the cell's genetic material.

Prokaryotic cells do not have a membrane-bound nucleus.

Bacteria and archaea are prokaryotic organisms.

Animal, plant, fungal, and many other cells are eukaryotic.

Organelles are specialized structures inside cells.

Mitochondria produce much of the usable energy in many eukaryotic cells.

Ribosomes build proteins.

The endoplasmic reticulum is involved in producing and processing certain proteins and lipids.

The Golgi apparatus modifies, sorts, and packages molecules.

Lysosomes contain enzymes that help break down certain materials.

Plant cells contain chloroplasts.

Chloroplasts perform photosynthesis.

Plant cells also commonly have a large central vacuole.

The cell wall provides structural support in plants and some other organisms.

Cells require energy to perform their functions.

Cellular respiration releases energy from nutrients.

In many organisms, glucose can be broken down to produce ATP.

ATP is a molecule used to transfer usable energy within cells.

Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy.

Plants, algae, and some microorganisms perform photosynthesis.

During photosynthesis, light energy is used to produce energy-rich molecules.

Carbon dioxide and water are important reactants in photosynthesis.

Oxygen is released during the photosynthetic process used by many plants.

Metabolism is the collection of chemical reactions occurring in an organism.

Some metabolic reactions build molecules.

Other metabolic reactions break molecules down.

Enzymes are biological catalysts.

They increase the rate of specific chemical reactions.

Enzymes are not usually consumed by the reactions they catalyze.

The function of an enzyme depends on its structure.

Temperature and pH can affect enzyme activity.

Extreme conditions can change the structure of an enzyme.

Proteins are large biological molecules made from amino acids.

The sequence of amino acids influences the structure and function of a protein.

Proteins perform many functions.

They can act as enzymes.

They can provide structure.

They can transport substances.

They can send and receive signals.

They can participate in immune responses.

Nucleic acids store and transmit genetic information.

DNA is a nucleic acid that stores genetic instructions.

RNA performs several important roles in cells.

DNA is composed of nucleotides.

A nucleotide contains a sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogen-containing base.

The bases in DNA include adenine, thymine, cytosine, and guanine.

DNA commonly exists as a double helix.

Specific base-pairing rules help the two DNA strands interact.

A gene is a segment of genetic material associated with a functional product.

Genes provide instructions for producing functional molecules or influencing biological traits.

The complete genetic material of an organism is its genome.

Chromosomes are structures that organize genetic material.

Different organisms have different numbers and arrangements of chromosomes.

DNA replication produces a copy of genetic material.

Replication allows genetic information to be passed to new cells.

Transcription produces an RNA molecule using DNA information.

Translation uses information in messenger RNA to help build a protein.

The flow of genetic information is often described as DNA to RNA to protein.

Mutations are changes in genetic material.

Some mutations have little effect.

Some mutations can alter biological function.

Some mutations can be harmful.

Some mutations can be beneficial in particular environments.

Genetic variation is important for evolution.

Cells can divide through different processes.

Mitosis produces genetically similar daughter cells in many situations.

Mitosis supports growth, repair, and asexual reproduction in some organisms.

Meiosis produces reproductive cells with reduced chromosome numbers.

Sexual reproduction combines genetic material from reproductive cells.

This creates genetic variation among offspring.

An organism's genotype is its genetic makeup.

An organism's phenotype includes observable characteristics.

The phenotype is influenced by genotype and environment.

An allele is one version of a gene.

Individuals may inherit different alleles from their parents.

Dominant and recessive patterns describe certain inheritance relationships.

A dominant allele can influence a phenotype when one copy is present in some genetic systems.

A recessive phenotype may require two copies of a recessive allele in simple inheritance patterns.

Many traits are influenced by multiple genes and environmental factors.

Genetics is the study of heredity and variation.

Gregor Mendel's experiments helped establish important principles of inheritance.

Modern genetics has expanded beyond simple Mendelian inheritance.

Evolution is the change in inherited characteristics of populations over generations.

Natural selection is one mechanism of evolution.

Individuals in a population vary.

Some variations can affect survival and reproduction.

Traits that increase reproductive success can become more common over generations.

Natural selection acts on existing variation.

Mutations and other processes can introduce genetic variation.

Genetic drift is a change in allele frequencies caused by chance.

Gene flow occurs when genetic material moves between populations.

Populations can evolve over time.

Individual organisms do not evolve during their own lifetime in the biological sense of changing their inherited genetic composition.

Evolution occurs across generations of populations.

A species is a group of organisms defined according to biological criteria.

The exact definition of species can vary depending on context.

Speciation occurs when populations become sufficiently different that new species arise.

Geographic isolation can contribute to speciation.

The fossil record provides evidence of past life.

Comparative anatomy can reveal similarities among organisms.

Homologous structures share evolutionary origins even when their functions differ.

Molecular biology can reveal similarities in DNA and proteins.

These lines of evidence support the theory of evolution.

Classification organizes organisms into groups.

Taxonomy uses shared characteristics and evolutionary relationships.

Modern classification often uses genetic evidence.

Ecology studies interactions between organisms and their environment.

An organism is an individual living thing.

A population is a group of organisms of the same species in an area.

A community includes multiple interacting populations.

An ecosystem includes organisms and their physical environment.

The biosphere includes the regions of Earth where life exists.

A habitat is the environment in which an organism lives.

A niche describes the role of an organism in its environment.

Food chains represent the transfer of energy through feeding relationships.

A food web shows interconnected feeding relationships.

Producers create organic matter using energy from sunlight or other sources.

Consumers obtain energy by consuming other organisms.

Decomposers break down organic material.

Energy flows through ecosystems.

Matter cycles through ecosystems.

The carbon cycle moves carbon through the atmosphere, organisms, soil, oceans, and geological systems.

The nitrogen cycle moves nitrogen through the environment and living organisms.

Water moves through the water cycle.

Populations are affected by available resources.

Competition occurs when organisms require the same limited resources.

Predation occurs when one organism consumes another.

Parasitism occurs when one organism benefits while another is harmed.

Mutualism occurs when both organisms benefit.

Commensalism describes an interaction in which one organism benefits while the other is not significantly affected.

Biodiversity describes the variety of life.

Biodiversity can refer to genetic diversity, species diversity, and ecosystem diversity.

Human activities can affect biodiversity.

Habitat destruction can reduce populations.

Pollution can damage ecosystems.

Climate changes can alter environmental conditions.

Conservation biology studies ways to protect biodiversity and ecosystems.

Plants are multicellular organisms that perform photosynthesis.

Roots anchor plants and absorb water and minerals.

Stems provide support and transport materials.

Leaves are important sites of photosynthesis in many plants.

The vascular tissues of plants transport water and nutrients.

Xylem transports water and minerals from roots.

Phloem transports sugars and other organic materials.

Plants respond to environmental signals.

Hormones regulate many plant processes.

Animals are multicellular organisms with specialized tissues and organs.

Animal bodies maintain internal conditions through homeostasis.

Homeostasis is the regulation of internal conditions.

Examples include regulation of temperature, water balance, and blood glucose.

The nervous system allows rapid communication through electrical and chemical signals.

The brain and spinal cord form the central nervous system.

Peripheral nerves connect the central nervous system with other parts of the body.

Neurons transmit information.

The endocrine system uses hormones to regulate biological processes.

Hormones are chemical signals released by specialized cells or glands.

The circulatory system transports substances throughout the body.

Blood can transport oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products.

The heart pumps blood through blood vessels.

Arteries generally carry blood away from the heart.

Veins generally carry blood toward the heart.

Capillaries allow exchange between blood and tissues.

The respiratory system exchanges gases.

In humans, oxygen enters the body through the respiratory system.

Carbon dioxide is removed as a waste product of cellular respiration.

The digestive system breaks food into molecules that can be absorbed.

Nutrients provide materials and energy needed by the body.

The immune system protects organisms from many harmful biological agents.

The immune system can recognize and respond to foreign substances.

Innate immunity provides general defenses.

Adaptive immunity can produce specific responses to particular targets.

Antibodies are proteins involved in certain immune responses.

Microorganisms are microscopic organisms or biological entities.

Bacteria are single-celled prokaryotic organisms.

Many bacteria are harmless or beneficial.

Some bacteria can cause disease.

Archaea are also prokaryotic organisms but differ from bacteria in important biological characteristics.

Fungi include yeasts, molds, and mushrooms.

Fungi obtain nutrients by absorbing materials from their surroundings.

Many fungi are important decomposers.

Some fungi form beneficial relationships with plants.

Viruses are infectious biological particles that require host cells to reproduce.

A virus contains genetic material surrounded by a protein structure.

Viruses can infect many types of organisms.

The immune system can respond to viral infections.

Vaccines can train the immune system to recognize specific biological targets.

Disease can result from many causes.

Pathogens include certain bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites.

Not all microorganisms cause disease.

The human microbiome consists of communities of microorganisms associated with the human body.

Some microorganisms contribute to digestion and other biological processes.

Biotechnology uses biological systems to develop products and technologies.

Genetic engineering modifies genetic material using biological techniques.

DNA sequencing determines the order of nucleotides in genetic material.

Modern biology uses technologies to study genes, proteins, cells, organisms, and ecosystems.

Biology is connected to chemistry because life depends on chemical reactions.

Biology is connected to physics because organisms use physical processes.

Biology is connected to environmental science because organisms interact with ecosystems.

Biology is connected to medicine because understanding biological systems helps explain health and disease.

Life is organized at multiple levels.

Molecules form cellular structures.

Cells form tissues.

Tissues form organs.

Organs form organ systems.

Organ systems work together within organisms.

Organisms interact within populations and ecosystems.

Biology studies life across all these levels.

The study of biology helps explain how living systems are built, how they function, how they change, and how they interact with the world around them.
